PlGF forms heterodimers with VEGFA that reduce angiogenic activation compared to VEGFA homodimers. The resulting dampened angiogenesis and consequent hypoxemia may trigger a reactive, dysregulated angiogenic signaling response that produces aberrant, leaky blood vessels rather than functional vasculature. PlGF increases with age (p<0.05) and is a primary driver of the aberrant angiogenesis (AA) principal component alongside VEGFR1.

PlGF shows inversely associated with executive function across all groups, and sex-by-age dependent associations with grey matter volume: positive in younger women, inverse in older women (strongest individual marker effect: partial eta-squared 0.031 for PlGF x Age x Sex on GMV).

This mechanism complements the sVEGFR1/sFLT1 VEGF-sequestration pathway -- both PlGF heterodimer formation and sVEGFR1 decoy reception reduce effective VEGFA signaling, but through distinct molecular mechanisms that converge on the same outcome: impaired vascular maintenance.

Extends [[sflt1-vegf-imbalance-associates-with-reduced-axonogenesis-pathway-activity-in-multi-cohort-somascan-data-after-vascular-comorbidity-and-batch-effect-correction]] -- PlGF adds a second mechanism of VEGF pathway suppression beyond sFLT1 sequestration.

Extends to [[PlGF may contribute to white matter injury through both vascular permeability and neoangiogenesis-neurodegeneration pathways creating mechanistic ambiguity in cross-sectional data]] -- the reactive aberrant angiogenesis triggered by heterodimer-dampened signaling is the molecular candidate for the neoangiogenesis pathway that competes with the vascular permeability explanation in Kern 2025.

Kern 2025 downstream consequences: elevated plasma PlGF (independent of heterodimer biology) directly associates with white matter free water (beta=0.21), WMH (beta=0.17), and CDR (aOR=1.67) in 370 older adults with cerebrovascular risk. SEM mediation shows FW explains 73% of PlGF-WMH and 26% of PlGF-CDR. These quantified imaging and cognitive consequences of elevated PlGF provide the clinical endpoint evidence for what the aberrant angiogenic state indexed by high PlGF produces in the brain.
