Non-linear principal component analysis of seven circulating angiogenesis markers in a two-center longitudinal cohort (N=435, mean age 74) identifies two stable, orthogonal components. PC1 ("aberrant angiogenesis") loads on PlGF, VEGFR1, VEGFA, and VEGFC and increases with age, particularly in women. PC2 ("vascular health") loads positively on bFGF, VEGFA, VEGFC and inversely on PlGF, VEGFR1, Tie2, decreasing with age in both sexes. Bootstrap stability is high (congruence coefficients 0.996 and 0.991 across 500 resamples).

The two-component structure separates a pathological angiogenic signature (driven by decoy receptor and placental growth factor) from a protective vascular maintenance signature (driven by bFGF). This decomposition is critical because individual markers show complex, sometimes opposing associations with brain outcomes -- the composite scores resolve these into interpretable dimensions.

MarkVCID imaging validation (Kern 2025): the PlGF-heavy aberrant angiogenesis dimension has direct imaging consequences in the MarkVCID cohort (N=370). PlGF associates with white matter free water (beta=0.21) and WMH (beta=0.17), and SEM shows FW explains 73% of the PlGF-WMH association and 26% of the PlGF-CDR association. This provides structural validation that what PC1 captures pathologically (aberrant angiogenesis) translates into measurable white matter interstitial fluid accumulation and radiological damage.
