The MSD V-Plex electrochemiluminescence platform used in Torres-Espin 2024 employs VEGFR1 antibodies that detect both the secreted soluble form (sVEGFR1/sFLT1) and solubilized membrane-bound VEGFR1 protein. This means plasma VEGFR1 measurements are a composite of decoy receptor (pathological) and membrane receptor (physiological) forms, introducing measurement ambiguity for mechanistic interpretation.

This limitation parallels the SomaScan aptamer specificity question for sFLT1 in the vascular-contributions-to-neurodegeneration analyses. Both platforms face the challenge of distinguishing soluble from membrane-bound receptor forms in plasma.

Connects to [[assay platform choice affects numeric cutpoint values for plasma biomarkers necessitating platform-specific clinical thresholds]] -- VEGFR1 isoform ambiguity is a platform-specific measurement limitation.

Connects to [[simoa quanterix batch effects introduce run-to-run variability that confounds cross-sectional plasma biomarker comparisons]] -- different VEGFR1 assay platforms (MSD V-Plex, SomaScan, Simoa) may capture different isoform ratios, complicating cross-platform comparisons.
