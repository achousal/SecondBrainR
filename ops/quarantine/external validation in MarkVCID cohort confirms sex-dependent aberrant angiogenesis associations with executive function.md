An independent validation sample from the MarkVCID dataset (N=80, age range 50-90, 37.5% female, varying cognitive impairment) partially replicates the primary findings. The PC1 (aberrant angiogenesis) loading pattern is confirmed, with partially similar PC2 (vascular health) loadings. Sex-dependent associations between AA and executive function replicate in stratified analysis.

The small validation sample (N=80) limits the power to detect the full range of sex-by-age interaction effects. Nevertheless, the structural replication of the AA component and its sex-stratified cognitive associations across independent cohorts with different recruitment criteria strengthens confidence in the findings.

Extends to Kern 2025 MarkVCID findings: in the full MarkVCID cohort (N=370, mean age 72), PlGF specifically associates with white matter free water (beta=0.21), WMH (beta=0.17), and CDR (aOR=1.67), and FW mediates 26% of PlGF-CDR and 73% of PlGF-WMH. See [[elevated plasma PlGF associates with greater white matter free water fraction in older adults with cerebrovascular risk]] and [[PlGF independently predicts worse clinical dementia rating score with an adjusted odds ratio of 1-67 per standard deviation increase]]. These PlGF-specific findings from the same consortium expand on the sex-stratified executive function findings by establishing a structural (FW-mediated) pathway between elevated PlGF and white matter injury.
