Plasma markers of angiogenesis provide diagnostic information for cognitive impairment that is independent of, or complementary to, AD neuropathological biomarkers. bFGF concentrations distinguish cognitively normal from MCI groups regardless of amyloid PET status. Aberrant angiogenesis (AA) composites differ between amyloid-negative and amyloid-positive MCI groups, capturing vascular contributions missed by amyloid markers.

Torres-Espin 2024 characterizes this as "a blueprint for investigation of plasma biomarkers of vascular neurodegenerative disease, with great promise for future diagnostics and clinical trials interventions." The external validation in the MarkVCID cohort (N=80) partially confirms the sex-dependent AA-executive function associations in an independent sample.
