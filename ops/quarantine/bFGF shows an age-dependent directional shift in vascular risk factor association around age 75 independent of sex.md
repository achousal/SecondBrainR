bFGF shows an age-dependent directional shift in its association with vascular risk factors: inversely correlated with the vascular risk factor component (vPC1: hypertension, hyperlipidemia, diabetes) in younger individuals, transitioning to a positive association around age 71-75. This shift is independent of sex and APOE status. bFGF consistently shows an inverse association with cardiovascular disease burden (vPC2) regardless of age or sex.

The directional shift may reflect a transition from bFGF as a marker of vascular health (higher bFGF = fewer risk factors in younger adults) to bFGF as a reactive compensatory response (higher bFGF = attempted vascular repair in older adults with accumulated vascular damage). The WMH association onset at age 71-72 coincides with this shift.

Connects to [[arteriolosclerosis is the most common microvascular lesion in hypertensive SVD and drives downstream white matter and parenchymal damage]] -- bFGF may attempt to compensate for arteriolosclerotic damage in older adults.
