Karvelas et al. 2024 studied 24 CADASIL patients and 24 age- and sex-matched controls using semiautomated white matter ePVS quantification. The CADASIL and control groups did not differ significantly in mean ePVS volumes, indicating that ePVS burden is not a reliable discriminator between case and control status at the group level. However, within the CADASIL group, higher ePVS volume was associated with greater WMH volume (beta=0.57, P=0.05) and higher CDR Sum-of-Boxes score (beta=0.49, P=0.04) using linear regression. This pattern -- no between-group difference but robust within-group associations -- argues that ePVS volumes index disease severity and progression rather than disease presence. This has direct implications for trial design: ePVS volume is most useful as a within-patient continuous severity measure or a longitudinal change marker, not a cross-sectional case identifier.

The WMH association is consistent with the mechanistic hypothesis that enlarged perivascular spaces reflect impaired glymphatic clearance or perivascular inflammation that promotes periventricular white matter injury -- both of which are established features of CADASIL pathophysiology via NOTCH3-driven arteriolar wall changes. The CDR-SoB association grounds ePVS in functional and cognitive outcome, making it a candidate intermediate phenotype linking structural vascular disease to cognitive status. Despite ePVS co-occurring with WMH and brain atrophy in CADASIL, [[no single plasma protein associates with all three CADASIL imaging measures indicating partially distinct pathological axes for ePVS WMH and brain atrophy]] -- indicating that these three structural measures are not interchangeable endpoints and should each be modeled with its own protein panel.

This claim connects directly to the ongoing question in [[cadasil-translational-characterization]] about which imaging measures track with disease burden. The finding that ePVS tracks with WMH within CADASIL but does not separate cases from controls parallels the finding that some SIMOA plasma biomarkers show elevations in CADASIL that track with white matter disease burden (see [[plasma simoa biomarkers are elevated in cadasil and track with white matter disease burden]]), but both require replication in larger cohorts to confirm they are NOTCH3-driven rather than generic vascular effects.
