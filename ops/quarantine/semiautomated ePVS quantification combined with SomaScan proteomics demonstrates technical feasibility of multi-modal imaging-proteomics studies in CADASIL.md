Karvelas et al. 2024 demonstrated that it is technically and logistically feasible to combine semiautomated white matter ePVS volume quantification with high-plex plasma proteomics (SomaScan, 7363 proteins) in a CADASIL cohort of 24 patients with 24 age- and sex-matched controls. Both modalities were applied in the same individuals, enabling direct imaging-proteomics correlation analyses and network-level hub protein identification. The semiautomated ePVS pipeline was novel at the time of the study and required no manual rater for each brain. The SomaScan assay required plasma samples collected in the same study visit.

This design pattern -- small but deeply phenotyped matched cohort with simultaneous high-plex proteomics and quantitative imaging -- is the closest existing template to the planned multi-modal CADASIL characterization in [[cadasil-translational-characterization]]. The study demonstrates that the bottleneck in such studies is not data generation (both modalities are now accessible) but analytical power: with n=24, associations are hypothesis-generating rather than confirmatory, and protein-level discoveries require replication before they can inform clinical decisions.

The methodological precedent also establishes that the SomaScan platform can detect CADASIL-relevant biology when paired with a specific imaging phenotype endpoint. For the VascBrain cohort, a similar design pairing ePVS quantification with SIMOA (targeted plasma panel) or, if resources permit, SomaScan, would enable direct comparison and potential replication of the CXCL8 and CCL2 hub findings in an independent sample.

Key design elements to replicate or improve upon: (1) semiautomated rather than manual ePVS segmentation for scalability; (2) pre-specification of the imaging-proteomics analytic plan before unblinding; (3) BH-FDR correction for the protein screen; (4) inclusion of brain parenchymal fraction and WMH volume as parallel imaging endpoints to enable the dissociation analysis described in [[no single plasma protein in the cadasil somascan dataset associates with all three imaging measures indicating distinct pathological axes for epvs-wmh-and-brain-atrophy]].
