When a plasma proteomics signature fails to replicate externally, the usual first response is to look for assay or cohort differences. But the asymmetry in the Keller 2024 results — late-stage signature replicates, early-stage does not — points to something more fundamental than technical variation.

Both signatures were discovered in the same dataset, using the same platform, the same analytical pipeline, and the same validation cohort. The difference is not methodological. The difference is biological: early-stage CADASIL patients are more heterogeneous than late-stage ones.

There are several plausible mechanisms. First, NOTCH3 variant diversity: Colombia has high-frequency founder variants that may cluster in patients with atypical NOTCH3 mutations (high GOM burden variants differ from EGF-domain mutations). Early-stage disease, where compensatory mechanisms are still active, might produce different plasma signatures depending on which specific variant is driving the pathology. Late-stage disease, where damage is extensive and inflammation is fully engaged, may converge on a common final pathway regardless of the initiating mutation.

Second, vascular comorbidity. Early-stage patients (typically <55 years) have had less time to accumulate hypertension, diabetes, and other confounders, but those who have them may show dramatically different metabolic plasma profiles from those who do not. Late-stage patients are older and more uniformly affected; their vascular comorbidity burden is more homogeneous.

Third, individual biology. The oxidative stress and heme pathway disruption that characterizes the early signature is sensitive to diet, exercise, sleep, and genetic variation in metabolic pathways — all sources of noise that are less relevant once the inflammatory cascade is fully engaged.

The implication for early CADASIL biomarker development is that discovery cohorts need to be larger and more diverse than what is sufficient for late-stage work. The fact that [[the late-stage CADASIL plasma proteomics signature generalizes to an external Colombian validation cohort while the early-stage signature shows only marginal external validity]] is not a failure of the study — it is a signal about the difficulty of the early detection problem.
