Individual angiogenesis marker associations with brain structure and cognition show small effect sizes (partial eta-squared 0.009-0.031) in the Torres-Espin 2024 cohort (N=435). The strongest individual effect is PlGF x Age x Sex on grey matter volume (0.031). APOE-stratified models show similarly small effects (0.011-0.015).

These small individual effects motivate the use of multivariate composites (aberrant angiogenesis, vascular health) rather than single markers for clinical applications. The NL-PCA composites integrate signals across markers, capturing shared variance that individual markers cannot. This aligns with the general pattern in plasma biomarker research where composites and ratios outperform single analytes.

Connects to [[differential diagnosis among dementia subtypes requires combinations of biomarker panels rather than single analytes]] -- effect size data confirms that vascular biomarker panels must be multi-marker.
