Karvelas et al. used the SomaScan assay to quantify 7363 plasma proteins in the 24 CADASIL patients and 24 controls. Linear regression of each protein against ePVS volumes followed by pathway enrichment analysis revealed two directionally opposite biological signals. Proteins positively associated with ePVS volumes -- indicating higher protein levels as perivascular spaces enlarge -- enriched for leukocyte migration and inflammatory signaling pathways. Proteins negatively associated with ePVS enriched for lipid metabolism pathways, suggesting that disruption of normal lipid homeostasis accompanies or precedes perivascular space expansion.

The leukocyte migration enrichment is mechanistically coherent with the established biology of perivascular spaces as immune surveillance corridors: enlargement may reflect increased trafficking of peripheral immune cells into the CNS parenchyma, consistent with the role of perivascular spaces as anatomical conduits for neuroinflammatory entry. The individual hub proteins identified in the ePVS network -- [[CXCL8-IL-8 is a hub protein in the CADASIL ePVS network and independently associates with white matter hyperintensity volume]] and [[CCL2-MCP-1 is a hub protein in the CADASIL ePVS network and independently associates with brain atrophy MMSE and executive function]] -- are canonical leukocyte chemoattractants that directly instantiate this pathway-level enrichment at the individual-protein level. CXCL8 recruits neutrophils and monocytes; CCL2 recruits mononuclear phagocytes through the CCR2 axis. Their co-emergence as network hubs is not coincidental -- it reflects the leukocyte migration enrichment signal at the resolution of individual molecules.

The lipid metabolism signal is less immediately interpretable but may reflect disruption of myelin maintenance or lipid-mediated BBB integrity pathways that are downstream of NOTCH3 arteriolar dysfunction. Notably, this negative enrichment converges with findings from a larger CADASIL proteomics dataset: [[complement dysregulation and lipid-peroxisome metabolism are the dominant pathway signals in late-stage CADASIL plasma proteomics]] identifies peroxisome-fatty acid metabolism as among the leading disrupted pathways in late-stage disease using an independent SomaScan 7k cohort (N=53). The directionality aligns: both studies implicate lipid metabolism disruption as part of the CADASIL plasma signal, suggesting this is a reproducible feature across imaging-anchored and disease-stage-anchored proteomics analyses rather than a cohort-specific finding.

This enrichment result positions ePVS in CADASIL as a structural readout of ongoing neuroinflammatory activity -- connecting the imaging phenotype to the molecular biology of immune trafficking. The leukocyte migration enrichment builds directly on the claim established in [[chronic neuroinflammation and microglial activation are central amplifiers in cerebrovascular disease progression not merely downstream consequences]] by providing proteomics-level evidence that vascular neuroinflammation is not only ongoing but structurally encoded in ePVS volumes in a monogenic SVD model. More specifically, it populates the inflammatory response arm of [[inflammatory response markers coagulation-thrombosis markers and circulating microRNAs are emerging peripheral biomarker classes for vascular cognitive impairment]] with concrete proteomics-derived candidates in a CADASIL imaging-proteomics paradigm -- converting a theoretical biomarker class into molecularly specified targets.

A mechanistic interpretation of how leukocytes reach perivascular spaces requires BBB compromise upstream. The pericyte dysfunction that characterizes CADASIL vasculature ([[pericytes are central regulators of bbb integrity and cerebrovascular homeostasis whose dysfunction contributes to both vascular and neurodegenerative disease]]) provides the cellular gate through which peripheral immune cells gain access to the perivascular compartment. Under this model, NOTCH3-driven pericyte and smooth muscle cell loss disrupts the barrier, allowing leukocyte extravasation, which enlarges perivascular spaces further -- a positive feedback loop encoded in the leukocyte-ePVS association.

The protein-level structure of the ePVS network also underlies the imaging-axis dissociation documented in [[no single plasma protein associates with all three CADASIL imaging measures indicating partially distinct pathological axes for ePVS WMH and brain atrophy]] -- the two hub proteins in this network (CXCL8 and CCL2) each associate with only a subset of the three imaging measures, confirming that the leukocyte migration enrichment signal does not generalize uniformly across ePVS, WMH, and brain parenchymal fraction.

Limitation: the n=24 CADASIL patients is small for an unbiased screen of 7363 proteins; the enrichment signal reflects pathway-level aggregation, and individual protein associations are likely underpowered. Pathway conclusions should be treated as hypothesis-generating pending replication in larger SomaScan or mass spectrometry proteomics cohorts.
