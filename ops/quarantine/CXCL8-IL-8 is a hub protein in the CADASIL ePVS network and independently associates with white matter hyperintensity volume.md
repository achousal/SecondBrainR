From the ePVS-associated plasma protein network, Karvelas et al. identified two central hub proteins: CXCL8 (CXC motif chemokine ligand 8, also known as interleukin-8) and CCL2 (C-C motif chemokine ligand 2, also known as monocyte chemoattractant protein 1). This claim concerns CXCL8/IL-8. As a hub protein, CXCL8 had the highest connectivity in the protein-protein interaction network built from the ePVS-associated proteins, indicating it is a central coordinator of the inflammatory signaling associated with perivascular space enlargement. Beyond network topology, CXCL8 plasma levels were also independently associated with WMH volume (beta=42.86, P=0.03).

This dual role -- network hub in the ePVS-associated proteome AND independent predictor of WMH volume -- positions CXCL8 as a molecular bridge between perivascular space enlargement and white matter injury in CADASIL. The ePVS-WMH structural link is established by [[ePVS volumes in CADASIL associate with WMH burden and cognitive severity within patients but do not differ from controls at the group level]]; CXCL8 provides the first candidate molecular mediator of that imaging-level association. The directionality is consistent with a model where peripheral immune cell recruitment through enlarged perivascular corridors releases inflammatory mediators that promote white matter damage -- a pathway directly grounded in the leukocyte migration enrichment reported in [[ePVS-associated plasma proteins in CADASIL enrich for leukocyte migration and inflammation pathways while negatively associated proteins enrich for lipid metabolism]].

CXCL8 is a canonical neutrophil and monocyte chemoattractant. Its co-hub status with [[CCL2-MCP-1 is a hub protein in the CADASIL ePVS network and independently associates with brain atrophy MMSE and executive function]] defines the core of the CADASIL perivascular inflammatory network, but the two chemokines diverge sharply in their outcome associations: CXCL8 predicts WMH (white matter injury) while CCL2 predicts brain parenchymal fraction and cognitive measures (atrophy and cognition). This dissociation is consistent with the broader finding in [[no single plasma protein associates with all three CADASIL imaging measures indicating partially distinct pathological axes for ePVS WMH and brain atrophy]] -- CXCL8 and CCL2 are each hub proteins for the ePVS network but track distinct downstream injury axes, suggesting they operate on different timescales or pathological cascades within the NOTCH3-driven arteriopathy.

Mechanistically, CXCL8-driven neutrophil infiltration through perivascular corridors could produce direct white matter injury through proteolytic and oxidative mechanisms, or could amplify the inflammatory cascade that [[chronic neuroinflammation and microglial activation are central amplifiers in cerebrovascular disease progression not merely downstream consequences]] positions as a self-sustaining driver of parenchymal injury. The perivascular anatomical context is critical: perivascular space enlargement in CADASIL reflects NOTCH3-driven arteriolar dysfunction and impaired glymphatic clearance; when those corridors are enlarged and CXCL8 is elevated, the combination implies both structural and inflammatory failure of the neurovascular unit -- the same unit disrupted by pericyte loss described in [[pericytes are central regulators of bbb integrity and cerebrovascular homeostasis whose dysfunction contributes to both vascular and neurodegenerative disease]].

CXCL8 has been implicated in BBB disruption and neuroinflammation in multiple cerebrovascular contexts, including stroke and SVD. This positions it within the neuroinflammation pathway of the mechanistic triad described in [[endothelial dysfunction blood-brain barrier disruption and neuroinflammation are the three core pathophysiological pathways underlying vascular cognitive impairment and dementia]], and specifically within the inflammatory response biomarker class identified in [[inflammatory response markers coagulation-thrombosis markers and circulating microRNAs are emerging peripheral biomarker classes for vascular cognitive impairment]]. Its emergence as a hub in a CADASIL-specific ePVS network represents a convergent validation of this inflammatory axis in a monogenic SVD model. However, causality cannot be established from cross-sectional associations at n=24; CXCL8 may reflect ongoing inflammation rather than driving it.

For the vascular inflammation axis examined under [[cadasil-translational-characterization]], CXCL8 is now a prioritized candidate for SIMOA-based or ELISA-based validation in larger cohorts. The sFLT1 work (see [[elevated-sflt1-in-vascbrain-simoa-plasma-correlates-with-cadasil-clinical-severity-and-white-matter-lesion-burden-independently-of-vegf]]) probes angiogenic dysregulation; CXCL8 probes the inflammatory chemokine axis -- these are complementary rather than redundant, each indexing a distinct arm of the VCID pathophysiological triad.
