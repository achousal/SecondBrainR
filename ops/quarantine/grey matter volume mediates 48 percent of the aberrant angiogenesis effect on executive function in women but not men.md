In women, grey matter volume (GMV) mediates 48% of the total effect of aberrant angiogenesis (AA) interacting with age on executive function (indirect effect p=0.01). The VEGFR1 x Age interaction shows an even stronger mediation path (p=0.004). In men, no significant mediation through GMV was detected (AA x Age p=0.56; VEGFR1 x Age p=0.602).

This sex-specific mediation establishes a causal pathway model: in women, angiogenic dysregulation -> grey matter atrophy -> cognitive decline. The absence of this mediation in men suggests either (a) a different mechanistic pathway from angiogenesis to cognition, (b) insufficient power given the weaker direct associations in men, or (c) genuinely sex-specific neurovascular coupling.

Extends [[neurodegenerative and cerebrovascular diseases have bidirectional pathogenic interactions mediated through shared vascular mechanisms]] -- provides specific evidence for the vascular -> structural -> cognitive chain in aging.

Connects to [[angiogenesis marker associations with brain structure and cognition are sexually dimorphic with directionality reversing around age 75 in women]] -- the mediation analysis provides mechanistic depth to the sex-dimorphic pattern.
