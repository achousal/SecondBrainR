A core question in CADASIL pathophysiology is how a mutation in a vascular protein eventually produces cognitive decline and dementia. The cell-type shift described in Keller 2024 provides a mechanistic bridge: disease starts in the vascular wall and propagates outward into the brain parenchyma via glial involvement. The vascular-wall initiation phase implicates mural cells including pericytes: [[pericytes are central regulators of bbb integrity and cerebrovascular homeostasis whose dysfunction contributes to both vascular and neurodegenerative disease]] -- pericyte dysfunction in NOTCH3-mutant vasculature is the cellular mechanism that converts early-stage endothelial and mural-cell pathology into the BBB breakdown and hypoperfusion that subsequently drive glial compartment involvement.

The single-cell transcriptomics integration maps the plasma proteomics signatures to specific brain cell types. Early-stage proteins are predominantly expressed in endothelial cells (VEGFR3, FN1, MGP, HPCAL1) and oligodendrocyte precursor cells (ENPP2). This is coherent: early CADASIL is a disease of the vascular wall, and the plasma proteins at this stage originate from the cells most directly affected — the endothelium and the smooth muscle/fibroblast lineage that maintains arteriolar architecture.

In late-stage disease, the cellular sources shift. The late-stage proteins map to mature oligodendrocytes (OMG, B3GAT1), astrocytes (BCAR3), T cells (KLRF1), and endothelial cells still involved (SEMA3B, TMEM132B, ABO). This shift from endothelial/fibroblast to astrocyte/oligodendrocyte is consistent with the pathological sequence: once the blood-brain barrier fails and white matter ischemia progresses, the glial compartment — which is responsible for myelin maintenance and neuronal support — becomes directly involved.

The T cell (KLRF1) contribution in late-stage is also notable: it places adaptive immune involvement at this stage, consistent with [[chronic neuroinflammation and microglial activation are central amplifiers in cerebrovascular disease progression not merely downstream consequences]] and connects to the complement dysregulation described in [[complement dysregulation and lipid-peroxisome metabolism are the dominant pathway signals in late-stage CADASIL plasma proteomics]].

This cell-type progression model is mechanistically important because it suggests that therapeutic windows depend on which cellular compartment is targeted. Early interventions targeting endothelial health may prevent the transition to glial involvement; late interventions targeting oligodendrocyte survival may slow progression once that transition has occurred.
