The diagnostic performance of plasma p-tau217 has been extensively validated in memory clinic and ADRC cohorts where patients present with cognitive complaints. French et al. 2025 extend this validation into a fundamentally different clinical setting: asymptomatic cardiovascular disease patients with no prior cognitive diagnosis. In 162 adults undergoing cardiovascular care, p-tau217 achieved AUC 0.94 (95% CI 0.88-0.99) for detecting MoCA-based cognitive impairment, with beta = -1.46 (95% CI -2.53 to -0.39, p < 0.01) in multivariate regression adjusted for age, sex, and education.

This finding is distinct from prior AUC estimates for p-tau217 in two important ways. First, the criterion is cognitive impairment defined by MoCA normative thresholds, not amyloid PET positivity. The semantic neighbor -- [[plasma p-tau217 achieves AUC 94-97 percent for amyloid PET classification in a community cohort enriched for vascular and metabolic comorbidities]] -- uses amyloid PET as the reference standard, making the two claims complementary rather than redundant. Second, the population is drawn from cardiovascular care rather than neurology or memory clinic referral pathways, meaning the base rates of cognitive impairment and the spectrum of vascular comorbidity differ substantially: [[29 percent of asymptomatic cardiovascular patients have undiagnosed cognitive impairment exceeding general population prevalence and revealing a systematic screening gap]] quantifies exactly how far outside the normal range these base rates fall.

The convergence of AUC values (~0.94) across such different populations and endpoints strengthens the case that p-tau217 captures a biologically meaningful signal rather than one optimized for a narrow clinical context. This is consistent with [[p-tau outperforms amyloid-beta for detecting AD pathology and has stronger concordance with CSF and PET reference standards across independent cohorts]], which established p-tau superiority across multiple validation phases; the French et al. result extends that superiority to a cardiovascular clinical context where amyloid-beta 42/40 fails entirely. The multivariate regression further demonstrates that p-tau217 contributes independent predictive value beyond demographic confounders, consistent with [[p-tau217 superiority over other plasma biomarkers for amyloid classification persists in cohorts enriched for prediabetes hypertension and kidney disease]].

The practical implication is that p-tau217 may serve as a screening biomarker in cardiovascular care settings where cognitive assessment is not routine, a possibility reinforced by the finding that [[55 percent of asymptomatic cardiovascular patients show elevated p-tau217 indicating high prevalence of occult tau pathology in a non-AD clinical population]]. The triage logic developed for memory clinics -- [[using plasma p-tau217 as a triage tool with sensitivity-specificity tuned cutoffs could reduce unnecessary csf and pet procedures in memory clinics]] -- extends to this population with equal or greater force, since the screening gap here is structurally larger and the pre-procedural cardiovascular assessment already provides a natural clinical contact point. The broader framing is supplied by [[cardiovascular patients represent an underscreened population for AD pathology and cognitive impairment warranting systematic biomarker assessment in cardiovascular care settings]], which makes the normative case for systematic biomarker deployment in this care setting.

One interpretive constraint applies: [[cognitive status is the primary stratification variable that determines whether p-tau217 double cutoffs meet confirmatory or screening test standards]]. In cognitively impaired populations, p-tau217 meets confirmatory test standards; the French et al. cohort contains a mix of impaired (29%) and unimpaired (71%) patients. Interpreting the AUC 0.94 for cognitive impairment detection as equivalent to confirmatory accuracy for amyloid classification would overstate its clinical standing -- this result supports a screening-triage deployment rather than direct diagnostic action without confirmatory follow-up.
