Beyond within-CADASIL associations, Karvelas et al. tested interaction-term models including disease status (CADASIL vs control) x ePVS volume terms alongside both groups. The interaction term was significantly associated with WMH volume (beta=0.57, P=0.02), CDR Sum-of-Boxes (beta=0.52, P=0.02), and MMSE score (beta=-1.49, P=0.03), with a marginal association with brain parenchymal fraction (beta=-0.03, P=0.07). This means the ePVS-outcome slope is steeper and statistically meaningful specifically in CADASIL, not in healthy aging controls, validating ePVS as a disease-state-contingent severity marker rather than a general marker of cerebral aging.

The interaction approach is methodologically important: it provides stronger evidence than a within-CADASIL-only analysis because it directly tests whether the ePVS-outcome relationship differs by disease status, rather than assuming it. The significant interaction for MMSE adds cognitive specificity -- ePVS volumes in CADASIL predict not only structural burden (WMH) and functional status (CDR-SoB) but also performance on a global cognition screen.

This result extends the claim established in [[enlarged perivascular spaces in cadasil are severity markers that track with wmh and cdr but do not distinguish cadasil from controls at the group level]], which documented the within-group associations. The interaction model result is the stronger causal-interpretive claim: the pathological relevance of ePVS is conditional on the disease context that CADASIL creates, pointing to NOTCH3-driven arteriolar dysfunction as the upstream cause that makes perivascular space enlargement consequential rather than incidental.

The marginal brain parenchymal fraction result (P=0.07) suggests that ePVS may also index early parenchymal atrophy in CADASIL -- a plausible chain where perivascular inflammation impairs glymphatic clearance, promotes white matter injury, and eventually contributes to volume loss -- but this pathway requires prospective confirmation.
