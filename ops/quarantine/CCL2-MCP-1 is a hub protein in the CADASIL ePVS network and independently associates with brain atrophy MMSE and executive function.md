The second hub protein identified by Karvelas et al. in the ePVS-associated plasma protein network is CCL2 (C-C motif chemokine ligand 2, also known as monocyte chemoattractant protein 1, MCP-1). CCL2 is a monocyte and macrophage chemoattractant that plays a central role in trafficking mononuclear phagocytes into sites of inflammation. As a network hub, CCL2 was central to the protein-protein interaction network built from ePVS-associated proteins. Beyond network topology, CCL2 plasma levels showed three independent outcome associations: decreased brain parenchymal fraction (beta=-0.0007, P<0.01), lower MMSE score (beta=-0.02, P<0.01), and increased Trail Making Test B completion time (beta=0.76, P<0.01).

This pattern -- CCL2 associated with atrophy (brain parenchymal fraction), global cognition (MMSE), and executive-attentional processing speed (Trail Making Test B) -- is clinically significant because it links a single inflammatory chemokine to both structural parenchymal damage and multi-domain cognitive impairment. Trail Making Test B is particularly sensitive to frontal-subcortical circuit integrity, which is a primary site of injury in CADASIL-related SVD. The convergence of CCL2 associations on exactly the cognitive domains most affected in SVD is mechanistically coherent.

Compared to CXCL8 (which associates with WMH and ePVS), CCL2 associates with parenchymal volume loss and cognitive impairment rather than white matter lesion burden per se. This suggests CCL2 and CXCL8 represent distinct axes of the inflammatory pathology in CADASIL: CXCL8 may track acute perilesional inflammation driving WMH accumulation, while CCL2 may track the downstream parenchymal and cognitive consequences of chronic monocyte infiltration. This distinction is relevant to biomarker panel design in [[cadasil-translational-characterization]] and directly instantiates [[no single plasma protein associates with all three CADASIL imaging measures indicating partially distinct pathological axes for ePVS WMH and brain atrophy]] -- CCL2 and CXCL8 are each ePVS hub proteins yet each tracks a different downstream imaging axis, providing protein-level evidence that ePVS, WMH, and brain parenchymal fraction capture partially distinct pathological processes.

CCL2 has been previously implicated in monocyte-driven BBB disruption and SVD-related cognitive decline in the broader VCID literature. Its emergence as a hub in a CADASIL-specific proteomics network, combined with its independent cognitive associations, makes it a priority candidate for validation via SIMOA or single-plex ELISA in the larger VascBrain cohort.
