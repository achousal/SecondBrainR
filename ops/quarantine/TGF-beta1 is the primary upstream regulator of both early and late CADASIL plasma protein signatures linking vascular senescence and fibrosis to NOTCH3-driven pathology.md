CADASIL is typically framed as a NOTCH3 gain-of-toxic-function disease: mutated NOTCH3 misfolds, accumulates as extracellular aggregates on arteriolar walls, and causes smooth muscle cell degeneration. What the Keller 2024 upstream regulator analysis adds is a bridge between this NOTCH3-centric story and a broader fibrotic vascular aging framework.

Network analysis of both the early-stage and late-stage plasma protein signatures converges on TGF-beta1 as the shared upstream regulator. This is not a coincidence of gene set topology — TGF-beta1 has three specific points of connection to CADASIL pathology. First, it drives vascular senescence: TGF-beta1 signaling is a canonical inducer of the senescence-associated secretory phenotype (SASP), consistent with the oxidative stress markers in the early signature. Second, it drives fibrosis: TGF-beta1 activates collagen production and extracellular matrix remodeling, which are pathological features of arteriolar thickening in CADASIL. Third, and most specifically, TGF-beta1 is known to bind to the extracellular domain of NOTCH3 — meaning that the mutant NOTCH3 aggregates that define CADASIL pathology are not merely structural deposits but may actively sequester TGF-beta1, dysregulating its signaling.

This convergence on TGF-beta1 repositions CADASIL from a NOTCH3 signaling defect to a TGF-beta1-mediated fibrotic vascular aging syndrome. The two framings are not mutually exclusive, but they suggest different intervention points. NOTCH3-centric approaches target the initiating mutation; TGF-beta1-centric approaches target the effector pathway that is engaged regardless of which NOTCH3 variant is present.

The fact that TGF-beta1 appears as the upstream hub for both [[heme biosynthesis and glutathione metabolism disruption are the dominant pathway signals in early-stage CADASIL plasma proteomics]] and [[complement dysregulation and lipid-peroxisome metabolism are the dominant pathway signals in late-stage CADASIL plasma proteomics]] suggests that TGF-beta1 may be a disease-spanning biomarker candidate, even as the downstream pathway patterns diverge by stage. This is actionable: TGF-beta1 itself or its canonical effectors (SMAD2/3 targets) could be measured across the full CADASIL disease course in the VascBrain cohort.
