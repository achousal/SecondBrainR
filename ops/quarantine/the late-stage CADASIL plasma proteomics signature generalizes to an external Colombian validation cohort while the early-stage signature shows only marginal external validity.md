External cohort validation is the standard stress test for plasma proteomics signatures: does the pattern discovered in one sample hold in an independent one? For the CADASIL signatures identified by Keller 2024, the answer depends critically on disease stage.

The late-stage signature (20 proteins centered on complement and lipid-peroxisome metabolism) generalizes. Applied to an independent Colombian CADASIL cohort, it shows significant enrichment (p=6.6e-3, permutation p=0.017). This is a meaningful replication across a genetically and demographically distinct population — Colombian CADASIL families often carry distinct NOTCH3 variants from European cohorts, so the biological consistency is not guaranteed.

The early-stage signature (16 proteins centered on heme biosynthesis and oxidative stress) does not replicate (p=0.055). This failure is not catastrophic — it is a marginal miss, not a null result — but it crosses the conventional threshold and cannot be claimed as a validated finding.

The differential generalizability has a biological interpretation, elaborated in [[early CADASIL signature failure in external cohort validation while late-stage succeeds suggests early disease has greater inter-individual heterogeneity that undermines plasma proteomics generalizability]]. Late-stage CADASIL converges on a stereotyped molecular phenotype — the complement cascade engages similarly regardless of NOTCH3 variant, vascular comorbidity burden, or geographic origin. Early-stage CADASIL is more heterogeneous: the oxidative stress and metabolic disruption that characterize it vary with individual biology, genetics, and possibly environmental exposures. A single early-stage signature cannot capture this range.

This finding should shape how early CADASIL biomarker discovery is approached. The field needs larger, more diverse discovery cohorts for early-stage work, rather than smaller cohorts with high internal validity. The late-stage signature, by contrast, may be ready for more systematic validation across clinical cohorts like VascBrain.
