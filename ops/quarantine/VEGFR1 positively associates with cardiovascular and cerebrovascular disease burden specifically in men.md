In the Torres-Espin 2024 cohort, plasma VEGFR1 positively associates with both cardiovascular disease burden (vPC2, partial eta-squared 0.029) and cerebrovascular disease burden (vPC3, partial eta-squared 0.009) specifically in men. This sex-specific vascular disease association suggests that VEGFR1/sVEGFR1 elevation in men reflects cumulative vascular injury rather than the age-dependent angiogenic compensation-to-pathology transition seen in women.

This finding has implications for interpreting sFLT1 (=sVEGFR1) in the SomaScan vascular-contributions-to-neurodegeneration analyses, which have not been sex-stratified.
